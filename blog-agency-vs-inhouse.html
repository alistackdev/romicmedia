In-House Team vs Hiring a Digital Media Agency

June 2026 8 min read Romic Media Team

This is one of the biggest decisions a growing brand faces, and there's no universally correct answer. It depends on your stage, budget, and how much control you want over day-to-day execution. Here's an honest comparison.

Building an In-House Team

Pros

  • Deep, exclusive focus on your brand only
  • Faster internal communication and approvals
  • Full control over hiring, culture, and direction

Cons

  • Hiring a full team (strategist, editor, designer, photographer) often costs significantly more in salaries, tools, and equipment combined
  • Skill gaps — one person rarely excels at strategy, editing, and design all at once
  • Slower to scale up or down based on project needs
  • Ongoing management overhead falls on you

Hiring a Digital Media Agency

Pros

  • Access to a full team of specialists (strategy, editing, design, photography) under one cost
  • Proven systems and processes from working across multiple brands
  • Easy to scale services up or down based on your needs
  • No equipment, software, or training overhead

Cons

  • Less day-to-day control compared to an internal team
  • Requires clear communication to maintain brand voice consistency
  • Quality varies significantly between agencies — vetting matters

What About the Cost?

Building a capable in-house team (strategist + editor + designer + photographer) typically means multiple salaries, software subscriptions, and equipment — often reaching a level that only larger, established brands can sustain comfortably.

A digital media agency typically operates on a monthly retainer or per-project basis, giving you access to an equivalent skill set without the fixed overhead of full-time salaries — making it a more flexible option for growing brands.

So Which Should You Choose?

Choose in-house if: you're a large, established brand with consistent, high-volume content needs and the budget to build a full specialized team.

Choose an agency if: you're a growing brand that needs professional-quality content and strategy without the overhead of building a team from scratch — and you want the flexibility to scale services as you grow.

Many brands also use a hybrid model — a small in-house person handling daily community management, paired with an agency for strategy, production, and campaigns.

The Bottom Line

There's no one-size-fits-all answer. The right choice depends on your current stage, growth goals, and how much you value flexibility versus full internal control. Whichever path you choose, consistency and quality matter more than which model you pick.
