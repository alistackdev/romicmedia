How to Write Hooks That Stop the Scroll

June 2026 5 min read Romic Media Team

You can have the best product, the best offer, and the best editing — none of it matters if your hook doesn't stop the scroll in the first two seconds. Here are the hook formulas we rely on for client content, and why they work.

1. The Curiosity Gap

This hook withholds information on purpose, creating a mental itch the viewer needs to scratch by watching further.

Example: "The one thing every successful Instagram page does that nobody talks about..."

2. The Bold Claim

A confident, slightly contrarian statement that challenges what the viewer already believes.

Example: "Posting every day is actually hurting your reach. Here's why."

3. The Direct Callout

Speak directly to your exact audience so they immediately know this content is for them.

Example: "If you run a restaurant and your Instagram looks dead, watch this."

4. The Visual Pattern Interrupt

Sometimes the hook isn't words at all — it's an unexpected visual: jumping straight into fast action, an unusual camera angle, or a sudden zoom. This works especially well in the first half-second before text even registers.

5. The "Mistake" Hook

People are more curious about what they're doing wrong than what they're doing right.

Example: "3 mistakes that are killing your TikTok reach right now."

What Makes a Hook Actually Work

  • Specificity beats vagueness. "Grow your Instagram" is weak. "Get your first 1,000 followers in 30 days without paid ads" is strong.
  • Say it, don't just show it. On-screen text reinforcing your spoken hook increases retention for viewers watching on mute.
  • Match the hook to the payoff. A hook that overpromises and underdelivers kills trust and hurts completion rate on future videos.

The Bottom Line

A strong hook isn't about being clickbait — it's about being clear and specific enough that the right person feels compelled to keep watching. Test different hook styles for the same piece of content and let the data tell you what resonates with your audience.
